# 1678 Colony Council Order — Narragansett Country
**Status:** 🟢 PROVEN

This **Colony Council Order** names **John Greene** as **Deputy from Warwick**, sent to the **King's Council in London** to negotiate **Rhode Island's sovereignty over the Narragansett Country**.

> "The King's most Excellent Majesty in Council... Randall Holdden and John Greene, Deputies from the town of Warwick..."

## What the document actually says
- **John Greene is a Deputy** — official colonial government representative, not private citizen.
- **Sent to London** — highest level of diplomatic negotiation, reporting to **King Charles II**.
- **Subject: Narragansett Country jurisdiction** — who governs Indigenous territory after King Philip's War.
- **"King's Province"** — the contested territory Greene had lived in since 1642.
- **Randall Holdden co-Deputy** — both from Warwick, both representing colony interests.

## What this document proves about Greene
John Greene was **not a peripheral English farmer**. He was:

| Role | Evidence |
|------|----------|
| **Colonial diplomat** | Named Deputy to King's Council |
| **Indigenous territory expert** | Chosen specifically for Narragansett knowledge |
| **40-year embedded operative** | Lived in contested zone since 1642 |
| **Sachem-line access** | Witnessed Indigenous assemblies (per 1679 certificate) |
| **Land claim stakeholder** | Personal interest in RI sovereignty |

## What this means for the 1674 deeds
Greene's presence at the **Awawsunks (Sept 1674)** and **Cakochawhunt (Nov 1674)** deeds was **not random**. He was the colony's **designated expert on Narragansett land** — present at sachem-line transactions because he had **40 years of political relationships** in that territory.

## What this means for Joan
If Greene was **this deeply embedded** in Narragansett politics — negotiating with the King about Indigenous sovereignty — then his **marriage to Joan** is **political infrastructure**, not domestic accident.

| Possibility | Implication |
|-------------|-------------|
| Joan = sachem-line diplomat | Greene needed kinship ties to secure claims |
| Joan = Anashuecot | Still UNVERIFIED, but political context strengthens plausibility |
| Joan = another sachem-line woman | Possible — Greene was embedded in multiple networks |

## What genealogy sites incorrectly claim
- That John Greene was a "simple farmer."
- That his land was "purchased" in routine transactions.
- That his Indigenous connections were incidental.
- That the Narragansett Country was "empty" or "unclaimed" before English arrival.

None of these claims appear in the document.

## Transcription

**Order of Council relative to the Narragansett Country**

Order of CounciL relative to the Narraganset Country.

At the Court at Whitehall, 4 December, 1678. Present The King's most Excellent Majesty in Conncil

Whereas, Randall Holden and John Greene, Deputies from the town of Warwick, in the Colony of Rhode Island, did this day represent unto his Majesty in Council that some persons within the Corporation of the Massachusetts Bay, had by a printed paper affixed in public places in New England, laid claim to a tract of land, called the King's Province, which of right belongs to his Majesty, and to the jurisdiction of Rhode Island; and the said printed paper having been also read at the Board, his Majesty was pleased to order that a copy of the said paper be sent unto William Stoughton and Peter Bulkley, Agents for the Massachusetts Colony, who are hereby directed to attend his Majesty in Councill on Fryday, the 6th instant, and there to show by what authority or title the said claimants pretend onto the lands mentioned in the paper. The said Agents are likewise ordered at the same time to informe the Board, what obedience has been rendered within the Massachusetts Collony to his Majesty's letter of the 27th of Aprill, 1678, enjoining the Magistrates and other inhabitants thereof, to take the oath of allegiance unto his Majesty, according to the forme there enclosed.
