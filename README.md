# The Joan Archive — Open Access Research Library

An archival advocacy platform and open-access research library dedicated to tracking the 17th-century lineages of John and Joan Greene of Quidnessett. This repository hosts the public interface, structured research indices, and targeted document request pipelines designed to isolate empirical facts from century-old genealogical contamination.

## ⚖️ The Core Forensic Directive

This project treats archival data through a strict compliance filter designed to pass intense peer, archivist, and legal-historian scrutiny:
1. **Verifiable Citations Only:** Every data point labeled `PROVEN` must map to an explicit, primary source document image.
2. **Firewalled Assertions:** Hypotheses, probable identifications, and unverified data layers must remain partitioned to protect project integrity.
3. **Collaborative Advocacy:** Document request mechanisms are intentionally structured to assist underfunded repositories by generating verifiable public demand metrics, justifying digitization funding.

## 🔍 Core Data Structures & Firewalls

### 1. The Timeline Status
* **John Greene Sr. Deceased Terminus:** Confirmed deceased prior to **September 25, 1685**, via the Edward Greene deed.
* **Joan Greene Survival Window:** Last recorded alive on **May 19, 1682**. Her status after this point remains strictly `UNRESOLVED`. The common assumption that she died before 1685 is flagged as unproven pending verbatim confirmation.
* **1688 Continuity:** The 1688 Devil's Purchase involves the exact indigenous kin group as the 1672 transaction (Womphoage, Ompamont, Quanuske, Mommocke), suggesting a transition of land recognition to **John Greene Jr.**

### 2. The Five-John Identity Firewall
To prevent lineage merging, this repository maintains rigid separation between five distinct individuals:
* **Quidnessett John Sr.:** Husband of Joan (active 1682); deceased by Sept 1685.
* **Surgeon John (Warwick):** Husband to (1) Joane Tattersall (d. ~1635), (2) Alice Beggarly (d. 1643/44), and (3) Phillippa (after 1668).
* **Newark John:** Independent NJ lineage documented in the 1666 Newark Town Book.
* **Stonewall John:** Indigenous builder deceased 1676; husband of Nawham.
* **East Greenwich John:** Associated with Abigail Wardwell and Mary Jeffery.

### 3. Contamination Index
* **The "Joan Beggarly" Myth:** Traced to La Mance (1904). Invalidated by historical letters showing Alice Beggarly died in January 1643/44—decades prior to the 1682 Quidnessett transactions.
* **The Bow-and-Arrow Misattribution:** Forensic analysis of the 1672 Fones Purchase reveals the top header is torn. The visible bow-and-arrow mark belongs to Mommocke, not Anashuecot. Historical records indicate John Greene used the block "IG" mark, while Joan utilized a distinct bow-and-arrow signature.

## 📦 Open Digitization Backlog Priorities

The repository tracks un-pulled primary manuscripts to coordinate targeted retrieval efforts from regional repositories (NK Land Evidence Vault, RI State Archives, RIHS):

1. **May 19, 1682 Pawtuxet Consent Deed (NK Land Evidence Vol 1):** The key secondary appearance of Joan giving free and voluntary consent to William Carpenter. *Status: High-Priority Un-pulled (Previous links to 1688 text were incorrect and have been purged).*
2. **September 25, 1685 Edward Greene Verbatim Check:** Required to analyze explicit text for any dower exclusions or mentions of Joan.
3. **1672 Fones Purchase Duplicate Audit:** Systematically assessing uncatalogued variant copies (e.g., RIHS Mss 1210) to crosscheck missing header losses.
4. **The Unfound Pile:** Evaluating un-digitized primary collections to isolate the context of local print press access proximate to these record windows.
