Joan, wife of John Greene of Quidnessett, appears twice in one month — March 24, 1681/2 and May 19, 1682 — then never again. Two appearances when English law needed her title is not an accident. It is evidence.

Why should this matter to you?
More history is rotting than is being used.

~5% digitized — Council of Europe / Fraunhofer ISI survey of 46 national archives: "To date, only a small proportion of documents, about 5%, have been digitized and users believe there is still great potential here." Source →
1-3% kept forever — National Archives: "Of all documents and materials created... only 1%-3% are so important... that they are kept by us forever." Source →
10+ billion pages undigitized — NARA holds "well over 10 billion pages... most having little to none of their hard copy documents digitized." Source → And "vast majority of unpublished archival material... has not been digitized and is not available online." Source →
Are we protecting our children's history? A child born in North Kingstown today cannot see the deed that defines her town's first land division without requesting a box in Providence. The top of page 22 — where Anashuecot's name and mark would be — is already washed away by water damage. If we don't photograph, transcribe, and host openly, the next flood or closed archive takes it forever.

Who else does this affect?
Narragansett descendants — If sachem women's title was translated as English wives' dower ("or to her mother if she survive"), their lineage is hidden in plain sight in land records.
Rhode Island landowners — If you own in Quidnessett / East Greenwich / North Kingstown, your chain runs through 1672 Fones Purchase and 1688 Devil's Purchase — same kin group, same witnesses. If those deeds are misread, your boundary history is wrong.
10,000 Greene descendants — La Mance (1904) added "Joan Beggarly" with no source. That error copied into Ancestry → FamilySearch → MyHeritage → Ancestry. One error, 10,000 trees.
Any child in RI — <5% accessible means 95% is on shelves, not search engines.

Scan Lab — How We Read A Damaged Page (Your Upload 1687.jpg)

1. Anashuecot = Awassecouse — PROVEN same person in same deed
Top p.22: Awashowet / Anashuecot (torn). Lower affidavit: "...sale... that the Indian Sachem Awassecouse made unto John Green..." Same transaction, same role, two spellings by same clerk = phonetic variation, not two people.

2. Bow-and-arrow mark — Does NOT = Anashuecot's mark
Visible marks p.22: Womphoage, Ompaniot, Mommocke (bow-like mark), Seecomp, Tyworasha, Absolom. Top where Anashuecot mark would be = TORN/MISSING. You cannot assign Mommocke mark to Anashuecot. Claim "Anashuecot had bow-and-arrow mark" = NULL from this primary. Origin Kettelle Papers Box 2; RIHS Mss 1210. Rocky Mountain transcript adding "his marke" = UNVERIFIED transcription layer.

3. Tocomiquian line — Careful reading
"...left as a gaurdan by his father Tocomiquian / Tocomminon Lamey should not deprive him..." — Proves Tocomiquian is father of Awassecouse (=Anashuecot). Does NOT prove father of Joan until Joan=Anashuecot proven. Held as internal open thread in /theory/open-contradictions/tocomiquian-guardianship.md, not homepage claim per Law 3.
