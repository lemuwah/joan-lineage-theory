The Core Question

Evaluating the Nature of Required Consent

Appearance 1 — March 24, 1681/2 (OS = March 1682 NS) Home-place: John Greene and Joan his wife grant 180 acres to sons Daniel and James, reserving a 30-shilling life estate to Joan, “or to her mother if she survive.” TIER 1 — SK Land Evid Vol IV[cite: 1]

Appearance 2 — May 19, 1682 Pawtuxet: John Greene and Joan his wife provide “free & voluntary consent” to William Carpenter, witnessed by John Wickes and Samuel Gorton. TIER 1 — UNPULLED original — previous 1688 link removed[cite: 1]

English form required a wife's consent to clear prospective dower rights to convey fee simple title. The execution of two signatures within 56 days demonstrates her signature was a structural requirement[cite: 1]. Whether this interest reflects standard English dower or a translated step to account for Narragansett matrilineal title remains an active query; the inclusion of the mother clause suggests the latter but requires definitive, corroborative proof[cite: 1].

⭐ How we read a damaged page — Primary Forensics

Same name, three spellings, same deed — and a mark that isn't hers

Proof: Anashuecot = Awashowet = Awassecouse — same person

Top header, damaged: ...of Awashowet [Anashuecot — head torn] Affidavit: "...that the Indian Sachem Awassecouse made unto John Green Henry Tibbits John Fones..." Fones index: Anashuecot, "Chiefe Sachem and heire" Three spellings, one role, same grantor, same deed. PROVEN same individual.

If Awassecouse = Anashuecot, and the affidavit records that Awassecouse was left as a guardian by his father Tocomiquian (“left as a gaurdan by his father Tocomiquian Lamey”), then Tocomiquian is the father of Anashuecot[cite: 1]. This line is maintained within researcher notes pending broader verification[cite: 1].

Disproof: Bow-and-arrow mark ≠ Anashuecot's mark[cite: 1]

Visible marks p.22: - Womphoage [his mark] - Ompaniat [his mark] - Mommocke [mark that looks like A / bow-and-arrow][cite: 1] - Seecomp his mark - Tyworasha his mark Top where Anashuecot mark would be = TORN / MISSING. You cannot assign Mommocke's mark to Anashuecot. Claim origin: Kettelle Papers Box 2; RIHS Mss 1210 — isolated compilation. Rocky Mountain transcript adding “his marke” = UNVERIFIED layer.
John Greene historically utilized a block "IG" mark, whereas Joan Greene utilized a distinct bow and arrow mark[cite: 1]. A damaged page can create a false attribution if a transcript compensates for a physical gap. We link every verified claim to its direct image source[cite: 1].

Contextual Implications

🪶 Matrilineal Realities

If Joan held title via indigenous lineages, the drafting of “or to her mother if she survive” may represent an English legal attempt to record an active indigenous land right within a traditional life-estate format[cite: 1].

🏠 Rhode Island Land Records

Local lines of descent cross-reference the 1672 Fones and 1688 Devil's Purchases, showing consistent tracking of the same kin groups (Womphoage, Ompamont, Quanuske, Mommocke) across generations[cite: 1].

🌳 Lineage Firewalls

La Mance (1904) assigned “Joan Beggarly” to this profile without supporting citations[cite: 1]. Historical letters indicate Alice Beggarly died in January 1643/44, decades prior to the 1682 deeds[cite: 1].

👧 Open Access

With massive physical holdings un-digitized, targeted record requests establish the vital metric data public archives need to request structural preservation grants[cite: 1].
