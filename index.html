Why Joan matters beyond genealogy: If Joan held Narragansett title, English law had to translate matrilineal property logic (“or to her mother if she survive”) into a life-estate clause. That's a rare written trace of two legal systems colliding — and it survived because someone asked for consent twice in one month.
The Core Question

Why does a woman who appears twice have to give consent?

Appearance 1 — March 24, 1681/2 (OS = March 1682 NS) Home-place: John Greene and Joan his wife grant 180 acres to sons Daniel (120a) and James (60a), reserving 30-shilling life estate to Joan, “or to her mother if she survive.” TIER 1 — SK Land Evid Vol IV

Appearance 2 — May 19, 1682 Pawtuxet: John Greene and Joan his wife — “free & voluntary consent” to William Carpenter, witnessed by John Wickes, Samuel Gorton, William Carpenter. TIER 1 — UNPULLED original — previous link to 1688 Devil's Purchase was error and removed

56 days apart. Alive at both. Then — nothing. No baptism, marriage, church roll, court testimony, estate inventory, dower claim, or gravestone before or after May 1682.

⭐ How we read a damaged page — Primary Forensics

Same name, three spellings, same deed — and a mark that isn't hers

Proof: Anashuecot = Awashowet = Awassecouse — same person

Top header, damaged: ...of Awashowet [Anashuecot — head torn] Affidavit: "...that the Indian Sachem Awassecouse made unto John Green Henry Tibbits John Fones..." Fones index: Anashuecot, "Chiefe Sachem and heire" Three spellings, one role, same grantor, same deed. PROVEN same individual.

If Awassecouse = Anashuecot, and affidavit says Awassecouse was left as guardian by father Tocomiquian (“left as a gaurdan by his father Tocomiquian Lamey”), then Tocomiquian is father of Anashuecot. Chain now probable — but kept in researcher notes footer per Law 3, no early promotion.

Disproof: Bow-and-arrow mark ≠ Anashuecot's mark

Visible marks p.22: - Womphoage [his mark] - Ompaniat [his mark] - Mommocke [mark that looks like A / bow-and-arrow] - Seecomp his mark - Tyworasha his mark Top where Anashuecot mark would be = TORN / MISSING. You cannot assign Mommocke's mark to Anashuecot. Claim origin: Kettelle Papers Box 2; RIHS Mss 1210 — isolated compilation, no primary shows Anashuecot bow-and-arrow. Rocky Mountain transcript adding “his marke” = UNVERIFIED layer.
Lesson: A damaged page can create a false fact if a transcriber fills the gap. This is why we link every PROVEN badge to the image.
