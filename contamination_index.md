# Contamination Index  
### Why genealogy sites get Joan Greene wrong

This page explains why Ancestry, YourRoots, MyHeritage, FamilySearch, WikiTree, and other genealogy platforms produce **false claims** about Joan / Joane / Anashuecot.  
None of these platforms use primary sources.  
All of them rely on **user-submitted trees**, auto-fill algorithms, and circular citations.

This index shows:
- how contamination happens,
- what the false claims are,
- and how to filter them out.

---

## 🟥 1. The Core Problem: Algorithmic Auto-Fill

Genealogy platforms “repair” missing parents by:
- guessing based on age,
- merging unrelated trees,
- forcing Indigenous women into English families,
- smoothing over contradictions,
- and inventing relationships when data is missing.

This is how **YourRoots ended up listing Joan as the daughter of Alice Daniels** — a relationship with **zero documentary support**.

There is **no** primary source linking Joan to Alice Daniels.  
There is **no** colonial record naming Joan’s mother.  
There is **no** English maternal line for Joan at all.

This is contamination.

## 🟩 3. What the Primary Sources Actually Say

These are the **PROVEN** anchors from the documentary record:

- **1672 Fones Purchase deed**  
  Names **Anashuecot** as “chiefe Sachem and heire properly.”

- **1677/8 Absolom affidavit**  
  States she was “**conveyed out of the country**,” father **Tocomminon**, brother **Washaucutt**.

- **1682 Pawtuxet sale**  
  “Joan” gives **“free & voluntary consent”** and signs **“her marke.”**

- **1682 home-place deed**  
  Life-estate clause: “as long as their **father or mother** should live.”

- **1703 Fones Record entry**  
  Re-enters the 1672 deed and 1677/8 affidavit 31 years later.

None of these documents mention:
- Alice Daniels  
- Cojonoquond  
- bow-and-arrow marks  
- English maternal lines  
- any English birth identity for Joan
