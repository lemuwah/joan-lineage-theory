3. **Pawtuxet Consent Execution (19 May 1682):** Independent legal agency record; Joan signs *"her marke"* for £10 sale to William Carpenter.

5. **Post-Mortem Possession:** Unprobated widow life-estate merges into sons' remainder titles upon her eventual death.

## 6. Linguistic / Methodological Lane

Narragansett has no masculine/feminine grammatical gender → *"his marke"* cannot be a faithful linguistic translation of an original Narragansett-language convention; must be scribe-added. Combined with Lisa Brooks's Weetamoo/Rowlandson scholarship, this gives real scholarly precedent for the mechanism the Anashuecot-as-woman argument needs. Fones's own hand shows gender-accurate *"her marke"* instances elsewhere in the same volume — closes the "generic scribal convention" explanation, but leaves open whether the original 1672 manuscript (pre-1703 entry) was itself accurate. 🟡
