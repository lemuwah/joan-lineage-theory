# 1672 Anashuecot Deed — Documentary Lifecycle Matrix
**Target Instrument:** Fones Purchase Deed (1 January 1671/72) & Associated Records  
**Grantor:** Anashuecot ("Chiefe Sachem and heire properly of ye land called quckeset")  
**Grantee Syndicate:** John Greene (first-named), Thomas Waterman, John Andrew, Henry Tybits, John Briggs, John Fones  
**Primary Source Anchor:** Rhode Island Land Evidence Deed #90; Fones Record (Arnold ed., 1894); RI State Archives  
**Status:** Tier 1 / Proven Primary Text (Belated Copy in Fones Record)  

---

## 1. Executive Summary & Purpose

The 1672 Fones Purchase deed is traditionally read as an isolated real estate transaction. However, operating within four competing colonial jurisdictions (Rhode Island, Connecticut, Massachusetts Bay, Plymouth), property records never acted alone. 

This matrix maps the complete **documentary lifecycle** of the 1672 deed across nine distinct administrative nodes. Reconstructing this chain identifies where corroborating paperwork (surveys, receipts, court acknowledgments, neighbor deeds) should exist, pinpoints survived echoes, and logs documented archival gaps.

## 3. Node-by-Node Detailed Breakdown

### Node 1: Pre-Transaction Negotiation & Political Context (1671–1672)
* **Administrative Context:** Negotiated in a jurisdictional "no-man's-land." Mass. Bay and Connecticut both claimed Narragansett Country; RI authority was contested.
* **Documentary Traces:** 
  * Real-time interlineation in deed body: *"being mistaken in the name of ye third it was Interlined, Tyecuecsha."*
  * Handwritten diplomatic memorandum: *"Namoush being ye Eldest son by misinformation was placed last wch will not be altered."*
* **Signatories & Offices:**
  * **Native Council:** Anashuecot (Sachem), Absolom ("Counsellor"), 2 brothers (Wampkegge, Ompamiatt), 3 sons (Seecomp, Tyecuecsha, Nammeash).
  * **Purchaser Syndicate:** Greene, Waterman, Andrew, Tybits, Briggs, Fones.
* **Source Anchor:** Fones Record, p. 1–3; RI State Archives.
* **Status:** **PROVEN** (Text preserved in 1703 copy).

---

### Node 2: Original Manuscript Drafting & Execution (1 January 1671/72)
* **Administrative Context:** Drafting of physical paper instrument. Scribal conventions applied (e.g., universal "his marke" notation across all Native signatories).
* **Witnesses Present:** Henry Greene (signed with mark), Absolom (signed in official capacity as "Counsellor").
* **Source Anchor:** Transcribed in Arnold (1894).
* **Documented Gap (GAP 1):** Location of the **original 1672 physical manuscript paper**. The surviving record is a copy entered into the town/colony record book decades later.
* **Target Archive:** Rhode Island Historical Society (RIHS MSS 629 / Updike Papers) and North Kingstown Loose Deeds Vault.

---

### Node 3: Magisterial Acknowledgment & Recording Fees (1672)
* **Administrative Context:** Colonial law required deeds to be acknowledged before a magistrate (e.g., John Greene of Warwick, John Sanford, or Randall Holden) and a recording fee paid to the town/colony clerk.
* **Source Anchor:** **UNANCHORED / GAP.**
* **Documented Gap (GAP 2):** Missing clerk endorsement on the 1672 instrument indicating before which magistrate it was sworn in 1672. No treasurer's fee entry found for 1672.
* **Research Action:** Audit Court of Trials records (1671–1673) for recording fee logs or magistrate docket entries for Narragansett purchases.

---

### Node 4: Private Custody & Wartime Transit (1672–1676)
* **Administrative Context:** During King Philip's War (1675–1676), Richard Smith's trading post at Cocumscussoc was garrisoned and surrounded; town records in the area suffered severe destruction or displacement.
* **Paperwork Survival Path:** The physical 1672 deed was held in private custody by the syndicate (likely John Fones or John Greene) rather than stored in a public town house, allowing it to survive the burning of Wickford/Quidnessett structures.
* **Source Anchor:** Inferred from 1703 record entry (Fones entry proves private possession post-war).

---

### Node 5: Post-War Ancillary Affidavit (1677/78 Absolom Testimony)
* **Administrative Context:** Post-war land title challenges forced purchasers to obtain supplementary sworn depositions clarifying sachem lineages and wartime displacements.
* **Instrument:** Sworn affidavit of Absolom ("Counsellor").
* **Key Statements:**
  * Names Tocomminon explicitly as Anashuecot's father.
  * States Mr. Browne was appointed guardian by Tocomminon.
  * Testifies Anashuecot was *"conveyed out of ye country"* following wartime disruption.
* **Source Anchor:** Fones Record, p. 6.
* **Status:** **PROVEN / PROBABLE** (Direct primary text in Fones Record).

---

### Node 6: Belated Official Bureaucratic Enrolment (1703)
* **Administrative Context:** John Fones, acting as clerk, enters both the **1672 Deed** AND the **1677/78 Absolom Affidavit** into the official record book 31 years after execution.
* **Bureaucratic Trigger:** Coincides with the settlement of boundary disputes, the re-establishment of North Kingstown administrative books, and colony-wide land commission reviews (1702–1704).
* **Source Anchor:** North Kingstown Land Evidence / Fones Record (1703 entry timestamp).
* **Status:** **PROVEN.**

---

### Node 7: Subsequent Chain of Title & Abutter References (1682–1715)
* **Administrative Context:** Neighboring deeds and family land divisions referencing the 1672 bounds.
* **Key Intersecting Instruments:**
  * **24 March 1682 Home-Place Deed:** John Greene deeds 180 acres of the 1672 tract to sons Daniel (120 ac) and James (60 ac), containing the life-estate clause for wife Joan.
  * **19 May 1682 Pawtuxet Consent:** Joan Greene provides separate consent to a £10 sale to William Carpenter.
* **Source Anchor:** F.L. Greene (1894, p. 10); Records of RI & Providence Plantations, Vol. VII, pp. 177–178.
* **Status:** **PROVEN.**

---

### Node 8: Cartographic Anchorage — The 1717/18 Quidnessett Plat
* **Administrative Context:** Formal survey and platting of Quidnessett lands by colony surveyors.
* **Geographic Metes & Bounds:** Map explicitly bounds *"John Green & Son 151 Ac"*, matching the exact physical acreage and natural landmarks (run/brook, salt meadow) described in the 1672 Anashuecot grant.
* **Source Anchor:** Rhode Island State Archives (1717–1718 Quidnessett Plat map).
* **Status:** **PROVEN.**

---

### Node 9: Archival Transcriptions & Modern Storage (1894–Present)
* **1894:** James N. Arnold transcribes and publishes *The Record of Old Warwick / Fones Record*.
* **1918–1919:** Louise Prosser Bates publishes analysis in *RIHS Collections* XI–XII.
* **Current Physical Custody:** Rhode Island State Archives (North Kingstown Land Evidence Series); Rhode Island Historical Society (RIHS Manuscripts).
