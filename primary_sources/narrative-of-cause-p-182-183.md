Manasses Mollafes, called and answered to the Name, being examined concerning Low Howland, kill'd at Pocafett Side (being an Englishman) and slaine or murdered by the Indians, and this Mollafes being charged or suspected to have a Hand in the Crime, answers, that he did not kill him, but being in the Woods, the Indians came and said such a one was killd, and offered to sell the Coate of the Person soe murdered or slaine, and that he the said Mollafes bought the Coate (of the said dead Man) for ground Nuts, and further faith, that it was one Quaiquomack killd the said Howland.
This Deponent, John Cook, aged about forty-five Yeares, testifieth, being at Punckateft, in the Midle of July, or thereabout, did ask of severall Indians, named as followeth: Woodcock, Matowat, and Job, whome they were, that killd Low Howland, the aforesaid Indians' Answer was, that there was six of them in Company, and Manasses was the Indian that fetcht him out of the Water. further this Deponent faith not.
This Deponent, John Brigs, aged thirty-five Yeares or thereabout, testifieth to the above written

Testimony, and also, that the said Manasses shot at Joseph Russell, as the Indians, first above named relate, and further this Deponent faith not.
Taken before me this 25th of August, 1676.
Wm. Cadman, Assist.
This Deponent William Manchester, aged twenty and two Yeares, or thereabout, being at Pocafett, asked of Peter Nonses, the Husband of Wetamoe, whose it was that killed Low Howland, his Answer was, that Manasses fetcht him out of the Water, and further faith not.
Taken before me, Wm. Cadman, Assist., Augt 25th, 1676.
Court adjourned until to-morrow Morning, eight of the Clock.
