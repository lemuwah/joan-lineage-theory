Transcription: East Greenwich Record, May 1677 p.2
[Page] 62
[Act] That the free men of the said Towne shall make and lay out convenient high wayes from the Bay up into the Country through out their whole Township as shall be convenient for the settlement of the Country above and about the said Township.
[Act] That the Deputy Governor, late president, of the afore-mentioned Court Marshall shall from the Secretary Mr Richard Bayly have a Copy of those writings relating to the tryall of the Indians & on the Colonys charge.
[Act] Whereas by reason of divers sorts of pay in this Colony passing between the Inhabitants thereof, and one and the same Comodity at more than one price, there hath arisen some disputes about the payment of certaine publick officers, and others who have their fees stated in a Table of fees and allowed by law, for prevention of which for the future: This Assembly have Ordained, and by the Authority thereof be it Enacted Ordained and Declared, That from and after the publication hereof, Every publick officer, hereafter Nominated Elected and chosen, to the place and office of Genll Acroider, Genll Soyer, Genll Treasurer, Genll Atturny and Genll Solicitor, or that now are in the places and offices aforesaid, shall have and take for and in the performance, of his or their said offices and places, but two third part of the ancient fees allowed in the Table of fees, in money or other pay as it is usually sold for money.
And further it is Enacted by this Assembly Enacted and Declared, that in every actionall cause, brought to Tryall in the Genll Court of Tryall, and Comitted to a Jury for them to bring in their verdict thereon the Jurors shall have twelve shillings in money for their service.
And it further Enacted and Declared by this Assembly and the Authority thereof that every Atturny or Councellor that shall draw up any Declaration or other writing, or pleading at the Barr, shall have but two third parts of his or their ancient fees allowed by the Table of fees aforesaid to be payd to him or them Employed in this service in money or other pay as aforesaid, And this act to stand and be in force any former law or laws to the Contrary Notwithstanding.
[Act] That Capt. Jenner or his order shall have one Barill of that powder now in the Armory of William Bromly's Custody, and the same he is to have in part of pay for the charge of the Garrison called the Kings Garrison at providence. And if lead bullets or shott be wanting there the said Capt. Jenner or order shall have on the same account not exceeding one hundred weight, and an order from the Clarke of this Assembly, unto Mr Stephen Arnold or whome shall order shall be a sufficient discharge to the said Mr William Bromly for what he doth deliver.
[Act] That the Clarke of this Assembly, who also was Clarke of the Assembly in may last shall for Recording the said two Assemblys acts, and for Coppys under the seale of the Colony to the four Townes Newport, providence, portsmouth and Warwick for which he shall receive in Money or Equivalent the sum of four pounds. And that the Towne of Newport shall pay thirty five shillings, the Towne of portsmouth thirty five shillings, the Towne of providence five shillings, the Towne of Warwick and the said Coppys shall be ready to be delivered unto such persons within thirty days of the Desolution of this Assembly.
[Act] That the Record shall within two months from the date hereof, sett up a true Coppys of all the laws made and Enacted in this Assembly of May last and of this present Assembly, Relating to the military affairs.
[Act] In octor for the & this Assembly is Desolved.
[New section — Court of Justices of the Peace]
Whereas there is a very great necessity for providing some speedy remedy for settling the Matter against the Country or Kings province from the injurious and illegall actions of some of Providence, It is therefore Ordered and Enacted by this Assembly and the Authority thereof, That there be a Court of Justices of the peace, consisting of the Deputy Governor, and six of the Assistants at least to be held at such place or places in the Kings province, in Narragansett as they shall see cause, and with the said Court shall see cause to enquire into, for the settling of the Inhabitants and such others as are by this Assembly admitted, and to apply such suitable remedies as to them shall seeme most convenient for settling the Inhabitants in peace and quietness: for doing and performing whereof the persons hereafter named, are invested with the full power of this Assembly to doe any act or acts, which they shall judge most convenient to be done or executed. And for the better governing and Abundance on the said Court; It is hereby Ordered that the Severall officers see Empowered on the said court, are Mr Richard Bayly & company, at Thomas Hazzard Genll &c. And it is also Enacted by this Assembly and the Authority thereof: That Major John Cranston Deputy Governor, Mr James Barker, Capt. Pelry Sanford, Mr Joseph Clarke, Capt. Samuell Wilbore, Capt. John Stillwell, 
## Capt. Green,
and Mr Samuell Gorton Assistants, or any seven of them of which the said Deputy Governor alwayes to be one; are the persons appointed and Authorized to hold the said Court, on the 15th instant and at such other times as he or they shall seeme convenient for doing, whereof and of all things they shall Judge requisite for preservation of his Majesties Interest, and Jurisdiction of the Jurisdiction of this Colony, with the said Narragansett or Kings province, as it is granted unto, and Established within the same by his Majesties Gracious Charter; and that of his Majesties Commissioners. This order or Transcript thereof under the seale of the Colony shall be their sufficient Warrant and discharge. And Ordered that the Serjeant, and Edmund Calverly by this Assembly appointed Constable to attend the Court aforesaid, bee also at the said Court in Narragansett.
Given and signed by order of the Genll Assembly, John Sanford Speaker of the Assembly.
Willington at Newport the 2 of May 1677
